# Remove specifics and convert to ambiguities

- Forget the specific organism. What general principle of organization makes it alive?
- Don't ask which molecule replicates. Ask what makes replication possible as a physical process
- Abstract from the specific pathway. What is metabolism in general? Sustained far-from-equilibrium chemistry. Now design for that
- Remove the species name. What kind of thing is it? An information-processing chemical system bounded in space. Is that alive?

# Repetition is a form of change

- Each cell division is a repetition. But the daughter is not the mother. The repetition is where evolution happens
- The same prebiotic experiment run a hundred times produces a different distribution each time. The repetition explores the possibility space
- Every generation of the synthetic organism is a repetition of the genome. But the regulatory state drifts. The repetition reveals the plasticity

# Reverse

- Instead of building up from chemistry to life, strip life down to chemistry. Top-down meets bottom-up. Where do they meet?
- Instead of asking how life began, ask how life could end permanently. What would it take? The answer defines what's essential
- Run the metabolism backwards. What does the cell look like as a consumer of products and producer of substrates?
- Instead of designing the organism for the environment, design the environment for the organism. Which is easier? That tells you which one life actually controls

# Short circuit

- Bypass the cell. Can the genetic circuit function in a cell-free extract? If so, what is the cell for?
- Skip the theory. Build the thing. The theory should explain what you built, not what you planned
