Inspired by Brian Eno & Peter Schmidt's Oblique Strategies ·

Oblique Strategies is a set of cards created by Brian Eno and Peter Schmidt in 1975. Each card carries a brief prompt; cryptic, lateral, sometimes contradictory; designed to break creative deadlocks. Musicians, artists, and designers have used them for decades to escape habitual thinking and approach familiar problems from unfamiliar angles.

The cards don't give answers. They disrupt the question. When you're stuck, a good oblique prompt reframes the problem just enough that a new path becomes visible.

This version adapts the original strategies for research and professional domains. Each card pairs the original prompt with a domain-specific interpretation; a concrete reframing for your field. Select a domain, draw a card, and sit with it for a moment before deciding whether it applies. The useful ones tend to be the ones that make you slightly uncomfortable.
